Recruiters live and die by reaching the right inbox. Whether you're sourcing candidates cold or following up with applicants, a bounced email isn't just a missed message, it's a missed hire.

## Why this matters more for recruiting than most use cases

Most email verification advice is written for marketers managing large lists. Recruiting is different in a few important ways:

- **Volume is lower, but stakes per contact are higher.** Missing one candidate because of a bad email address can mean losing a strong hire to a competitor who reached them first.
- **Addresses are often gathered manually.** Sourced from LinkedIn profiles, resumes, referrals, or guessed using common corporate email patterns, all of which are prone to errors.
- **Speed matters.** Top candidates get multiple outreach messages. If your first attempt bounces and you don't catch it for days, you've lost your window.

## Where bad data creeps in

### Guessed email patterns

A common sourcing tactic is guessing a corporate email format, `firstname.lastname@company.com`, based on a known pattern at that company. This works often enough to be useful, but it also produces a steady stream of addresses that simply don't exist, especially for employees who left or whose company uses a different format than expected.

### Manually copied addresses

Pulling an email address from a PDF resume or a LinkedIn "Contact info" section introduces room for transcription errors, extra spaces, misread characters, or outdated addresses from a previous job.

### Old contact databases

Recruiting CRMs accumulate candidate records over years. People change jobs and email providers constantly; a candidate record from two years ago has a real chance of having a dead email address by now.

## How to verify without slowing down your workflow

Recruiting moves fast, so verification needs to fit into the flow rather than become a separate chore:

### Before sourcing outreach, verify candidates individually

If you're about to send a cold outreach message to a sourced candidate, run the address through a quick check first. It takes seconds and saves you from outreach that goes nowhere, or worse, bounces in a way that makes you look careless to a candidate you're trying to impress.

### Before a bulk sourcing campaign, verify the whole batch

If you're sending to a sourced list, say, 200 candidates pulled from a sourcing tool using guessed email patterns, verify the entire batch first. This is where bulk verification earns its keep: upload the list as a CSV and get back a report flagging which addresses are valid, invalid, or risky before you spend your outreach budget on dead ends.

### Periodically clean your candidate database

If your ATS or CRM has accumulated candidate records over time, run a periodic check on stored addresses, particularly for high-value passive candidates you might want to re-engage later.

## What a good result tells a recruiter

When checking a sourced or guessed address, the most useful signal is a clear valid/invalid/risky result with a reason, not just a pass/fail. A "did you mean" suggestion is particularly useful here, since guessed patterns often produce near-misses that are one character off from the real address.

## The takeaway

In recruiting, every dead email is a missed shot at a candidate. Verification doesn't replace good sourcing, it makes sure the sourcing you've already done actually reaches someone.
