You hit send, and a chunk of your list bounces back. If this keeps happening, it's not bad luck, it's almost always one of a handful of fixable causes.

## What a bounce actually means

A bounce happens when an email fails to reach its destination. There are two kinds:

- **Hard bounces**, permanent failures. The address doesn't exist, the domain is invalid, or the mailbox has been deleted.
- **Soft bounces**, temporary failures. The mailbox is full, the server is down, or your message was too large.

Hard bounces are the ones that do real damage to your sender reputation. A high hard bounce rate tells mailbox providers you're not managing your list carefully, and that's exactly the kind of behavior spam filters are built to catch.

## The most common causes

### 1. You're sending to an old list

Email addresses decay. People change jobs, abandon accounts, or let domains expire. Industry estimates put natural list decay at around 20-30% per year. If you haven't sent to a list in six months or more, assume a meaningful chunk of it is dead.

### 2. The list was never verified at signup

If your signup form accepts any string with an `@` in it, you're collecting typos, fake addresses, and disposable emails right alongside real ones. Without verification at the point of collection, your list starts out dirty and only gets dirtier.

### 3. You imported or purchased a list

Purchased and scraped lists are notoriously unreliable. They're often outdated, full of spam traps, and were never verified in the first place. Sending to one of these without verification first is one of the fastest ways to damage a sending domain.

### 4. Typos in manually entered addresses

Sales teams and support staff manually type email addresses into CRMs constantly. A single transposed letter, `gnail.com` instead of `gmail.com`, produces an address that will bounce every time.

## How to actually fix it

### Verify before you send, not after

The fix isn't to send and see what bounces, that's reactive, and it still costs you the reputation hit. Run your list through a verification check before the campaign goes out. Bulk verification tools accept a CSV, check every address for mailbox existence and risk, and return a report you can use to clean the list before sending.

### Verify at the point of collection

Add verification to your signup forms so bad addresses never make it into your database. Catching a typo or a disposable address at signup is far cheaper than discovering it mid-campaign.

### Watch your sub-status, not just valid/invalid

A good verification report doesn't just say "invalid." It tells you why: mailbox not found, domain has no mail server, disposable address, or syntax error. That detail helps you spot patterns, like a specific form or import source that's consistently producing bad data.

### Remove risky addresses, not just invalid ones

Some addresses aren't outright invalid but are still risky to send to, role-based addresses, catch-all domains, or known spam traps. A bounce-focused cleanup only removes the obviously dead addresses. A risk-focused cleanup protects your reputation more broadly.
